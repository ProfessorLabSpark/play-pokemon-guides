# 1. Age Divisions Always Exist (Even in One Pod)
Even in a unified pod, TOM still treats:
- Juniors  
- Seniors  
- Masters  

…as separate divisions for:
- Round count  
- Standings  
- Prizing  
- Championship Points  

Pairings may cross divisions; standings and prizing never do.

---

# 2. Swiss Rounds Are Based on the Largest Division (with 2 or more players)

Players in a division → Swiss Rounds  
1 → 0 rounds (auto‑1st)  
2–3 → 2 rounds  
4–8 → 3 rounds  
9–16 → 4 rounds  
17–32 → 5 rounds  
33–64 → 6 rounds  
65–128 → 7 rounds  

Event round count = the highest number required by any division with 2 or more players.

Example:  
2 Masters → 2 rounds  
8 Seniors → 3 rounds  
14 Juniors → 4 rounds  
Event runs 4 rounds.

---

# 3. Drops Do NOT Change Round Count
Once Round 1 begins:
- Round count is locked  
- Drops do not reduce rounds  
- A division dropping to 1 player mid‑event does not recalc rounds  

---

# 4. Tiebreakers (How TOM Actually Calculates Them)

## MW% (Match‑Win Percentage)
TOM uses per‑round scoring:
- Win = 1  
- Tie = 0.5  
- Loss = 0  
- Bye = ignored  

MW% = average of per‑round values  
MW% cannot go below 25%.

---

## OMW% (Opponents’ MW%)
OMW% = average of each opponent’s MW%  
Each opponent’s MW% is floored to 25% before averaging.  
Byes are ignored.

---

## OOWP (Opponents’ Opponents’ Win Percentage)
OOWP = average of each opponent’s OMW%.

---

# 5. Mental Shortcuts
Think of each round as:
- Win = 1  
- Tie = 0.5  
- Loss = 0  

Then:
- MW% = your average  
- OMW% = average of your opponents’ averages  
- OOWP = average of their opponents’ averages  

This matches TOM’s behavior.

# 6. Prizing Rules (Division‑Based)
Prize pools are calculated per division.

Prize Pool = number of players in division × packs per player

Example (24 players, 2 packs each):  
Masters (2) → 4 packs  
Seniors (8) → 16 packs  
Juniors (14) → 28 packs  

---

# 7. Prize Distribution Models

## Method A — Participation + Top Cut (Nice Method)
- Every player gets 1 guaranteed pack  
- Remaining packs go to top finishers  

## Method B — Pure Top Cut (Typical Method)
- All packs go to top finishers  
- No participation packs  

Both are legal if:
- Announced before Round 1  
- Consistent within the division  
- Not based on age

# 9. Expanded Prize Pool Model (No Participation Packs)
Use this when:
- Each kid contributes 1 pack  
- No participation packs  
- Goal: about 50% of players receive prizes  

Steps:
1. Prize pool = number of players  
2. No participation packs  
3. Expand Top Cut to ~50% of players  
4. Apply proportional model  

Examples:  
8 players → Top 4  
12 players → Top 8  
16 players → Top 8  
20 players → Top 8  
24 players → Top 12 (optional)

# 12. Summary
- Divisions determine rounds and prizing  
- TOM determines tiebreakers  
- Unified pods only affect pairings  
- Prize pools are per division  
- Proportional models scale cleanly  
- Expanded Prize Pool Model gives ~50% of kids packs with no participation packs
